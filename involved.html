How to Get Involved in Data Carpentry

We're excited to get Data Carpentry off the ground and would love participation and engagement at any level or types. What can you do to get involved?

  1. Get notified of what’s going on with Data Carpentry
    • Follow our blog
    • Follow us on Twitter @datacarpentry
    • Check out our web site (well, you're here already). The website will be updated with new events though.
  2. Be involved in discussions on Data Carpentry
    • Join the discuss list
      http://lists.idyll.org/listinfo/dc-discuss
      Please sign up if you're interested in hearing more about Data Carpentry or want to be involved in lesson development or instructing.
  3. Become an instructor
    • If you are already a Software Carpentry certified instructor, you are a Data Carpentry certified instructor. We share the same training program. If you are a Software Carpentry instructor and would like to also be a Data Carpentry instructor, please let us know. We will also be setting up a system to figure out who is interested in teaching what between SWC and DC in the near future.
    • If you want to become an instructor and feel comfortable in at least two of the topic modules (spreadsheet, R or Python, SQL, shell), Greg Wilson is continuing to run train the trainer programs. Sign up to be notified of upcoming training opportunities http://software-carpentry.org/pages/register.html
    • Are there a lot of people at your institute or region interested in becoming instructors? contact admin@software-carpentry.org about running a train-the-trainers at your location.
    • If you want to see what instructing for Data Carpentry is all about, be a helper at a local DC or SWC workshop. Join the announce list to be notified of those opportunities.
  4. Help develop materials

    We're currently developing our lesson materials in GitHub.

    • If you're familiar with GitHub head on over to the our repo and check out the lessons and submit pull requests or issues
    • If you're interested in helping but new to GitHub, let us know and we can help with getting you started
    • Try teaching one of the lessons as a workshop for your colleagues. Submit issues on github on what worked and didn’t work or issue pull requests
    • Want to propose a new module or work on a new domain? We’re working with SWC to develop lesson templates, so new lessons can follow a standard format. Can’t wait for the template? Get in touch with us and tell us what you’re thinking about and we’ll help with the development.
  5. Host a workshop
    • Interested in hosting a workshop - get in touch!
      We don't want to commit to running them without knowing we'll have the capacity. If you're interested in hosting one though, please get in touch so we can start coordinating and know who's interested.
    • Don’t know if you want to run a Data Carpentry or Software Carpentry workshop? We’re partners! Let us know your workshop audience or questions or concerns and we’ll help you pick the right workshop for your group.
